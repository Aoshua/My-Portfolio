Academic Experience


Weber State University

I began to attend Weber State University part time when I was 16 years old (actual classes, not only concurrent enrollment), and in 2015 I graduated with my Associate in Science degree a week before I graduated from high school. I am grateful to WSU for all of the excellent learning opportunities it has provided me. I am currently a full-time student studying to receive my bachelor’s degree in Computer Science. Weber State University has phenomenal faculty and staff who truely put forth an effort to see their students succeed.

I am involved in Weber State University’s chapter of American Computing Machinery (ACM). Recently, I was elected treasurer. This past Easter, we hid plastic eggs in peoples’ yards as a fundraiser for our activities. Our activities at are geared towards giving fellow students in-depth learning opportunities and tours of different businesses and organizations within the field of technology. These activities help students discover and refine their interests as well as establish connections with professionals around the state.

I also work at Weber State University in its Writing Center, as a tutor. You will see more information about this on my “Work Experience” page. The Writing Center is a place where students can come to get help with any form of writing assignment they have. There, tutors help students to improve their grammar, organization, and clarity of expression. Students may also receive instruction on how to effectively conduct research and discover their writing style. Working as a tutor in the Writing Center has broadened my worldview because I have been able to meet various students with different cultures. In this way, the Writing Center connects students of all backgrounds with an open and friendly environment.


Northern Utah Academy for Math Engineering and Science

I graduated with my high school diploma from the Northern Utah Academy for Math Engineering and Science (NAUMES). It is this early college high school that helped me unveil my passion for learning. The excellent teachers and counselors of NUAMES pushed me to exceed in all that do. It is because of their partnership with Weber State University that I am there today. The teachers instilled in me a love for learning and the discipline necessary to excel in academia and my career. My time at NUAMES showed me how my choices and my work can truly make a difference in society.


Additional Learning Opportunities

The summer of 2013 I attended a class in computer drafting at the Davis Technical College (DATC). There I expanded upon the drafting concepts that I had learned my Sophomore year at NUAMES.

I have been a part of the Boy Scouts of America (BSA) ever since I was of age. With BSA I learned many different things, from the basics of astronomy to how to spend a night in the forest. However, the some of the most valuable lessons I learned are to serve others, to protect the environment, how to lead and when to follow, and the importance of planning.

From October 2015 to October 2017 I served as a missionary for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints in Northern Argentina. Morally, this was one the greatest learning experience I have ever had. It was a truly unique experience to forget about myself for two years and focus on the well-being of others. I met many friends, had crazy experiences, and saw many miracles that I will not forget. I am thankful to God for that time that I had to serve in Argentina, and I recognize His help in my studies and in my work.
