Poetry

I also really like to write poetry. I have written many poems, but, for the sake of space, I'll just include my favorite two:


No Slave to Self

You are not a slave to yourself
When you find that your mistakes got you in a bind
And you see yourself as the greatest enemy that there could ever be;
The beast that must be battled to expound upon your extensive existence

Think again.
You are not a feral animal,
Not just another mammal
The self can be tamed; it alone can be changed
So lift up your chin; find the power from within

You see, this hostility
Can only blast bitterly between your psyche
It’s your own mind that you must bind
In hopes to find a taste of the sublime
And to set yourself free from frustration,
To see beauty in the unsightly

If you simply unify your divided eyes
Make them synchronize to detect the lies that kept you
Locked in disguise, you’ll find the peace of mind that’s
Been there the whole time.
What is love?

Something that cannot be seen nor heard
As elusive as a dream or bird
No, you can’t paint it
Can’t frame it
Can’t box it
Or even say it.

Something so incredibly small
Yet fills the immensity of it all
It lifts fallen hands
Breaks the strongest of bands
Makes kings fall to their knees
And stutter out “Please.”
It could heal us
Or save us
Could kill us
Or enslave us.

Something unmeasurable.
Laps to the moon couldn’t do it justice
It is unpredictable
Where does it start?
Where does it end?
The heart's art cannot be penned.
Where does it come from?
Where does it go?
I’d give it all away to know
To have it stay with me
And kiss these battered knees
As I stutter “Please.”
“Oh darling, please.”
